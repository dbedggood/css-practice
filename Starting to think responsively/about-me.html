I Am a Developer

I used to be a computer science student at the University of Auckland. There was so much to learn that I felt I was supposed to appreciate, but I just couldn't. The truth is, a lot of it didn't feel useful to me and it really wasn't. I don't want to be a computer scientist, but there weren't other options for me wanting to get into software development.

This doesn't mean I did badly, I just found most of it incredibly boring which in turn made it difficult at times to motivate myself to do any work. I spent most of my time hanging out with friends, playing video games or working on (actually practical) personal software projects.

My Last Semester at University

In my last semester I didn't have much left to do. I took my final stage 3 paper, and then 3 other stage 1 papers just to fill out my degree planner. This gave me a little more time on the side to work on myself and line up a job for after I finish studying.

I worked on a few more projects, learned a few new skills, attended a couple hackathons and made a few more friends. However, there were (and still are) hardly any jobs out there for junior developers like me, and all the graduate programmes start at the beginning of next year. This made things really hard for me, I wasn't successful in my job hunt and I felt like I would never be able to find a job.

I Still Haven't Found a Job

I've been living off of Jobseeker Support (which has been incredibly helpful) as I apply to every developer job I can. It's really difficult because there are actually so many jobs out there, but it feels as if 99% of them require at least a couple years of commercial experience.

However difficult it may be, I know that I am trying my best. Which is why I have forgiven myself for not being able to find work straight after University. I'm still young and there is no real rush to start my career, I could always wait until the graduate programmes start early next year. I am incredibly grateful to have people that support me and I hope that I can make them proud sooner than later.

When I'm not applying for jobs, I'm working on developing my skills and growing myself to be more attractive to potential employers. This site and the others are an example of me trying to improve my proficiency in CSS. I'm doing this by reviewing the fundamentals and learning how create responsive websites and more without any CSS frameworks.

Something else I'm working on is a website for my lovely girlfriend, Rena (pictured above). I created it with React, Redux and Firebase. The layout is made with a CSS framework called Materialize. I'm planning to revamp the UX once I master pure CSS.
